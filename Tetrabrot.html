The Tetrabrot

In multicomplex dynamics, the Tetrabrot is a 3D generalization of the Mandebrot set. Discovered by D. Rochon in 2000, it can be interpreted as a 3D slice of the tricomplexe Multibrot set \(\mathcal{M}_3^2\).

The purpose of this note is to explain different mathematical algroithms that enable to draw the Tetrabrot set.

Divergence-Layer algorithm

There are different algorithms to generate pictures of the Tetrabrot. With the tricomplex numbers, the algorithms use the tricomplex function \(f_c(\eta) := \eta^p + c\) where \(\eta\) and \(c\) are tricomplex numbers and \(p \geq 2\) is an integer. The basic trick to generate the Tetrabrot is to use explicitly its definition:

A point \(c\) belongs to the Tetrabrot if, and only if, the sequence of iterates \( \left( f_c^{n} (0) \right)_{n= 0}^{\infty}\) stay bounded.
