About me

My name is Pierre-Olivier Parisé. I'm a Ph. D. student at Laval University (QC, Ca) under the supervision of Thomas J. Ransford. I will defend my thesis this summer. I am the owner of a Alexander-Graham Bell Graduate scholarship from NSERC and granted a 2 years-long graduate scholarship from FRQNT.

I graduated in 2017 from Université du Québec à Trois-Rivières. My supervisor was Dominic Rochon. I have worked on multicomplex dynamical systems and studied the Mandelbrot sets in the setting of the multicomplex numbers.

You can contact me by email

Research interests

My main research interests are in the field of complex analysis, including topics in geometric function theory, in operator theory and spaces of holomorphic functions, and in holomorphic dynamics and its generalizations to higher dimensions. The recent results of my research analyze connections between summability theory and Banach spaces of holomorphic functions in the unit disk.

Teaching

I have seven years of experience and training in teaching since the beginning of my career. I am convinced that an engaged student will learn and retain the material of a class more efficiently.
